01 — Research Interests

What I Study

🎮

Affective Task Switching & Cognitive Flexibility

Investigating how the brain dynamically switches between emotional and non-emotional goals. Using intracranial recordings, I study how task context (emotion vs. gender) and cognitive control (switch vs. repeat) interact to shape neural representations and behavior.

⚡

Single-Unit Encoding & Mixed Selectivity

Characterizing how individual neurons encode multiple task variables simultaneously (e.g., emotion × task transition). I focus on mixed selectivity, nonlinear encoding, and population-level representations in regions like the amygdala and pulvinar.

🧠

Thalamocortical & Limbic Circuit Dynamics

Exploring how the pulvinar and amygdala contribute to flexible cognition and emotional processing. I aim to understand how these regions coordinate during task switching and how they support adaptive behavior.

💻

Neural Data Analysis & Computational Methods

Developing analysis pipelines for spike trains and iEEG data, including time-frequency analysis, GLMs, decoding, and representational analyses to link neural activity with behavior.

Single-Unit Spike Sorting & Analysis

Developed and maintained an automated spike-sorting pipeline using Osort, combined with manual curation for high-quality unit isolation. Performed downstream analyses including firing rate dynamics, inter-spike interval (ISI) distributions, and population-level representations to study neural activity during behavioral tasks.

02

iEEG Preprocessing Pipeline

Designed and implemented a scalable pipeline for preprocessing intracranial EEG recordings, incorporating artifact rejection (bad channel detection, line noise filtering), referencing (bipolar), event alignment (TTL-based), and time-frequency analysis for neural decoding and statistical modeling..

MATLABSignal PreprocessingVisualization

Affective Task-Switching Task-Design

Modified and extended an existing affective task-switching paradigm by developing a controlled variant to isolate emotional interference effects. Implemented TTL-based event synchronization and generated structured behavioral and metadata pipelines to ensure precise alignment between neural recordings and task events.

Organized multimodal datasets into a unified, analysis-ready format to enable efficient data access, visualization, and cross-session analysis.
